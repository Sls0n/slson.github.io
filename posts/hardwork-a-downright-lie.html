2  | Hardwork — A downright lie!


4-5 minutes

"Hardwork makes you successful & rich."


This statement is a downright lie that is deeply rooted in our minds. Inculcating hard work into us has been a constant theme throughout history. Why are there hardly any examples of hard-working laborers being rich if it is so paramount? However, there are abundant examples of the rich gaining exorbitant profit merely by exploiting them.

"Having been indoctrinated with something does not ensure its validity. Thus, it is not logical to regard something as true merely because it has been indoctrinated into us."

We recklessly trust what we are indoctrinated with because, as a child, our brain cannot discern right and wrong. Thus, we still follow countless ridiculous & absurd beliefs.

The hard workers who do all the work are paid only a tiny fraction of what the wealthy earn. The one who works hard gets stuck in a rat race with all the daily drudgery and tedious work. Meanwhile, the rich reward themselves with fancy cars & travel around the world for all the hard work they did not do. Even the mere thought of buying a fancy car might seem practically impractical for the workers.

Nevertheless, knowing all this, why do we willingly relinquish our hopes and dreams just for a mediocre job? Because we are inculcated to do so? Because it is the right thing to do? I am not blaming riches for this. We are wholly accountable for our misery. No one forced us to work for the riches, right? And yet here we are whining about how we are exploited, showing hate out of righteous indignation, and condemning all the time.

People blame others/something for their misery to delude themselves into thinking they are not accountable for it. Blaming is easy and might feel good, but all it does is imperceptibly trap us in our self-delusional bubble, making us incapable of ameliorating our misery. Playing the victim or self-victimization is something we do as a coping strategy.

Nevertheless, still, I admit that hard work is a significant aspect of acquiring success. You cannot expect to become wealthy just by resting on the couch and watching Netflix the whole day just because a wannabe writer on the internet said so. In the initial paragraph, I discussed how hard workers working for the rich are taken advantage of by insidious means. Regardless, we must work tenaciously to get to the point where we can exploit those hard workers ourselves by being rich. We are all desirous & greedy creatures, after all.

So let us get back to working hard. As I said, hard work is one of the significant aspects of acquiring wealth. However, the type of hard work we should do is more than just hard work. There is a subtle yet vast difference between hard work and perseverance/persistence. Let us take the instance of James & Jimmy.

James was a hardworking man. He desired to become a musician. James worked relentlessly but noticed less progress than he anticipated. He began to regard learning music as a gamble for his career since he had reached nowhere. It completely drained his energy. So he chose to abandon learning music and do a simple job instead, which would pay him way less than what he would have achieved if he had been steady. Though he had a rigid working mindset & attitude, he lacked persistence. Thus, he could not endure the learning process leaving him with no alternatives. What he should have realized was that learning is a steep curve. He would have seen substantial improvement if he had not backed off so quickly. In a steep curve, you will not notice much improvement in the initial years, but the improvement will exponentially increase later.

However, Jimmy, on the other hand, was also interested in music. He was not as hardworking but was persistent, unlike James. Jimmy did not witness notable progress in the initial years either, but he did not let that obliterate his ambition of becoming a musician. He kept on moving forward slowly but steadily. Eventually, he noticed his skill advancing exponentially to the point that he began getting multiple offers. This example of Jimmy & James depicts the vast distinction between working hard and being persistent.

Okay, so persistence & perseverance is the key to becoming wealthy? No! Sorry to break it to you, but solely hard work will not let you anywhere. Let us assume it is just one-tenth of the thing that matters. There are countless other factors. Luck, Privilege, and Choice are some significant factors in getting rich. Luck is not tangible, so we think of it as something we lack authority over. We do not have control over our serendipity, but we make it unnoticeably for ourselves. The more discipline we have, the more luck we accumulate. The choices we make in our life have a profound influence on our future, but we are completely oblivious when we make them. Even a trivial choice will have a drastic unintentional effect on our life. This phenomenon is often called the "ripple effect," and choices have a ripple effect.
