3  | The unapparent perils of Self-Victimization


4-5 minutes

"We blame. "


I mean, No shit? We're human, it's what we do. What's more ludicrous is that our brain delightfully devours every lies we try to feed it, it must really like being lied to. Have you noticed how blatant lies in your head slowly morph into oblivious truth? It's practically impractical to not fall for your own shameless lies. I used to think that the brain is hard to deceive, but boy I was utterly unaware of how easy it is. Constantly lying to ourselves leads to a state of self-delusion, causing a distorted view of actuality. With time, we become more accustomed to accepting our own lies. With our own lies, we become convinced that we are the victim and have been denied justice. This is precisely why playing the blame game is a go-to resolution to feel good and avoid responsibility. This is exactly the weird incentive behind being a victim, even though we are the ones who commenced the inevitable fuckening. Self-victimization turns one into a perpetual victim, denying accountability and guilt for their own apparent mistakes. Here are some examples of it:

"I am poor, but it's not my fault! The whole game is rigged. Fuck capitalism!"

"I got into an accident. I was driving thoroughly like a good law-abiding citizen, but all of a sudden, this clown came out of thin air and crashed right into my ass!"

"There's no way I failed this test. Even lord can't fathom how possibly an outright genius like me could have failed. It's merely the paper maker's fault. He made the questions tougher than a titanium alloy. Why would anyone do that?"

Okay! I reckon now you have a solid grasp of the blame game with the above examples. If you don't, maybe it's time to get a brain scan and see if there's anything funky going on up there. Or have self-proclaimed intellectuals of Reddit & twitter explain to you like you're a toddler or something. I mean, the examples I gave were impeccable, clearly not my fault. I might as well be speaking to my imaginary girlfriend now. At least she exists in my imagination, unlike your brain. Just kidding, this was another bonus example for all of you. But if you still don't get it, I won't waste my time explaining it to someone whose IQ is lower than the temperature in Antarctica. Just kidding again, or am I?

These examples show what most of us do when faced with adversity - we divert it away, either by blaming or by being the victim. The relation between blaming and being the victim is convoluted yet so simple. By blaming others for your problem, you become the victim. You are essentially saying that you are not responsible for your own circumstances and that you are at the mercy of someone else's actions or behaviors.

People blame others/something for their misery to delude themselves into thinking they are not accountable for it. Blaming is easy and might feel good, but all it does is imperceptibly entangle us in our self-delusional bubble, making us incapable of ameliorating our misery. Playing the victim or self-victimization is something we do as a coping strategy to momentarily forget our problems, and constantly doing so exposes us to the unapparent perils of it.

A constant sense of victimhood traps you in a state of perpetual indignation. You won't strive for change anymore since it's clearly not your fault. You'll be too engaged in finding something to condemn in order to boost your self-righteousness. Blaming is as compulsive as drugs, which creates a massive dopamine surge in the brain due to which you want to do it more and more and more and well, more.

Let's take an instance of you arriving at the office late, is it your fault? Oh hell yeah it is... I mean, it's not. Wtf! It's clearly the busy traffic. "Dang it, boss, I literally got stuck in that mess for an hour. I am so unlucky, ughh". And the funniest part is that you're probably more convinced by your own lie than your boss is, which then leads to you becoming the victim. Blame all the time, and you'll find yourself cursing gravity for your downfall one day. I mean if it wasn't for gravity then you wouldn't fall, right? Or maybe it's the newton's fault for inventing gravity. Or maybe it's the fault of the person who invented newton. Or maybe it's the fault of the person who invented the person who invented newton. The possibilities are endless. You can blame anything and everything for your problems, but the truth is that you are solely responsible for every problems and you are the one to be blamed.

One of the most powerful roman emperors, also a stoic philosopher, Marcus Aurelius, once said: "Choose not to be harmed - and you won't feel harmed. Don't feel harmed - and you haven't been." You can't control everything that happens to you, but you can control how you choose to react to it. Choose to be a victim, and you'll entangle yourself in a perpetual state of victimhood. Or choose to acknowledge your mistakes, admit the fact that it's only you who can fix it regardless of who is at fault, and you'll be able to keep on consolidating yourself. You can either bury your head in the sand and live a life of self-delusion or you can embrace the fact that you are constantly going to find yourself getting fucked over by trivial problems. If you choose the latter, always remember that trivial problems are trivial. Either live as a victim or live as a victor. The choice is yours.
